PROGRAM DESCRIPTION


The Bachelor of Science in Psychology is an undergraduate program that aims to provide students with a strong foundation in psychological theories and concepts fundamental in delving into human behavior. As such, it aspires to develop students that are analytical, effective communicators, people-centered, technologically adept, ethical, empathic, and resilient.


Through the utilization of inter and multidisciplinary approaches, and employing flexible learning modalities, it seeks to produce competitive graduates with strong service orientation, leadership, and organizational skills; equipped in creating significant contributions in the field of Psychology locally and abroad, and well-prepared for further studies in the discipline and in other allied professions (such as education, medicine, and law).

PROGRAM LEARNING OUTCOMES (PLO)

By the time of graduation, the students of the BSPSY program shall have the ability to:

1. Demonstrate the capability to discuss and analyze the major theories and concepts in Psychology;

2. Evaluate and apply the methods of psychological inquiry in building knowledge on local culture and context;

3. Deduce and apply psychological theories and methods in personal and professional settings;

4. Exhibit the capability for self-reflection and independent learning in graduate education or in a professional context;

5. Exemplify professional and ethical behaviors in research and practice in Psychology;

6. Display the ability to relate appropriately with colleagues, clients, and others in diverse cultural settings;

7. Apply the proper administration of psychological assessments and evaluation; and

8. Utilize proper research tools and methodologies in appropriate epistemological and contextual underpinnings.

PROGRAM OBJECTIVES (PO)

The learning objectives of the program are achieved through the:

1. Introduction and fostering mastery of the fundamental theories and concepts in Psychology and its branches;

2. Developing of innovative learning paradigms such as webinars, simulation exercises, immersion, and the like, wherein students can demonstrate their ability to apply diverse psychological theoretical viewpoints and concepts in real-world settings;

3. Opening of avenues for students to delve into the tools, and techniques in psychological experimentation, research, and multidisciplinary approaches;

4. Analysis and Discussion of ethical and acceptable practices in the field of Psychology, with emphasis on effective communication, leadership, and organizational behavior; and

5. Integration of the moral, legal, ethical responsibilities, and societal impact of the psychology profession in their coursework.
