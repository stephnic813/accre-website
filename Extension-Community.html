Extension and Community Involvement
PUP offers extension services through a process that applies the understanding of critical needs, challenges, and possibilities; thus, ushering in transformation, self-reliance, and empowerment to the greater community. To effectively serve the community, PUP under the Office of the Vice President for Research Extension Planning and Development (OVPREPD) established an Extension Management Office (EMO) and formulated its manual as approved by a BOR Resolution No. 741, a series of 2010.

CSSD-HAGKANLUNGAN
The extension services program run by the College of Social Science Department (CSSD) in collaboration with the Sociology, Psychology, Economics, Cooperatives, and History departments is known as CSSD-HAGKANLUNGAN. Its stated purpose is "to promote an integrated and interdisciplinary community extension services program as one college, one university, and one nation." The CSSD-HAGKANLUNGAN provides the following extension services:
  1. Training and Development Program
  2. Resource Speaker on:
    1. Cooperative management standards
    2. Training of Trainers
    3. Economics and statistical updates
    4. Historians and tourist landmarks orientations
    5. Social Analysis and Awareness Seminar including gender equality
    6. Psychological-related seminars (like stress management, anger management, and others)
  3. Collaborations and partnerships for conferences (local, national, and international)
  4. Adopt a community program
  5. Relief operations and other community needs assistance
PsyKalinga
As one of the Departments under the CSSD, the Bachelor of Science in Psychology Program (BSPP) wholeheartedly supports the university's and CHED's goals and vision. As a result, it takes part in offering college-level extension services. The BSPP formulated its Extension Program called "PsyKalinga" patterned after and subsumed under the CSSD-HAGKANLUNGAN. The Program's extension services cover a range of subjects that are connected to the study of psychology, a discipline in which both the faculty and students have received relevant instruction and training. The Department's extension program also considers the obligations placed on the academe under the Implementing Rules and Regulations of the Mental Health Act (R.A. 11036) and other pertinent statutes.
Extension Brochure

The Bachelor of Science in Psychology Program (BSPP), being one of the Departments under the CSSD, fully believes in and supports the mission-vision of the University and CHED. As such, the vision and mission of the Psychology Department is to develop an extension flagship program dubbed as PsyKalinga that is fully functional and responsive to the needs of its beneficiaries.

Extension Partners
The LEVEL-UP CIW (Correctional Institute for Women)

The Polytechnic University of the Philippines (PUP), through the Department of Psychology, signed a Memorandum of Understanding (MOU) with the Correctional Institution for Women (CIW), in response to its commitment to provide an array of extension programs and psychological services. Leading an Empowering Valuable Life: Uplifting and Protecting CIW extension initiative is part of the University's Life Skills Program (LSP), which provides psychological services to officials of institutions catering to the vulnerable sectors.
